What does FraudShield do?

1
You submit a transaction with 8 features describing it (amount, time, account age, etc.)
2
A RandomForest ML model (100 decision trees, trained on 10,000 synthetic transactions) outputs a fraud probability between 0% and 100%
3
The decision engine applies risk thresholds and returns a verdict:
≥ 80% → BLOCK ≥ 50% → REVIEW < 50% → APPROVE

What each parameter means

💰
Amount
The transaction dollar value. Amounts over $300 are a fraud signal — fraudsters try to maximise what they steal quickly.
🏪
Merchant Category (0–9)
Encoded store type. 0 = groceries, 4 = electronics, 7 = wire/cash, 9 = luxury. Some categories are higher risk than others.
🕐
Hour of Day
What time the transaction happened (0–23). Purchases at 2–4am are suspicious — your card owner is probably asleep.
📅
Days Since Last Transaction
How long the account was quiet before this purchase. A dormant account suddenly making purchases = possible stolen card.
⚡
Transactions in Last 24h
Number of purchases today. More than 6 suggests velocity fraud — someone is burning through a stolen card as fast as possible.
🌍
Cross-border (isForeign)
Is the transaction from outside the account's home country? A domestic account suddenly buying abroad is a classic red flag.
🗓️
Account Age (days)
How old the account is. Brand new accounts (under 30 days) making large purchases are disproportionately fraudulent.
